What Happened: The Gozrans (worshippers of Gozreh, god of nature/weather) haven't forgotten the fracking or the boat that was seized from their allies. They aren't just sending angry badgers; they’re using localized weather magic. For the last two weeks, a localized, localized, highly unnatural squall of freezing, stinging rain has been parked directly over the ruined fort.

The Consequence: The fort is a miserable, muddy swamp. The already-ruined roofs are leaking constantly, and keeping fires lit is a struggle. Wood rot is setting in fast, and the Pharasman/Iroran medical duo have been working overtime just to prevent trench foot and pneumonia from decimating the slumfolk. A Gozran messenger bird arrived with a waterlogged scroll: “The earth was bled. The stone was cracked. Abandon this blight on the land, or the sky will wash it away.” The Decision: Infrastructure & Retaliation.

The players brought building supplies, but you can't roof a keep in a magical hurricane.
Do they need to send an expedition to hunt down the Gozran druid/cleric maintaining the ritual nearby?
This also creates an urgent ticking clock for the other issues: the tensions in Event 1 & 2 are infinitely worse because everyone is cooped up, freezing, and soaking wet.
By layering the Gozran Squall over the top, you force the players to deal with the political issues inside the fort while the physical environment itself is actively trying to kill their people!
